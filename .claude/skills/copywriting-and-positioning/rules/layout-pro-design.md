## Invest in professional design — graphics quality is a credibility signal

**Impact: MEDIUM** — Your visual presentation is one of the fastest credibility shortcuts the prospect uses. Especially with audiences who don't know you yet, graphic quality functions as a proxy for company quality. *"If their landing page looks this rough, what does the product look like?"*

This is peripheral-route processing (`psych-attitude-routes.md`) at work — the prospect isn't carefully evaluating the design; they're absorbing it as a quality signal in a half-second. Sloppy alignment, ugly typography, low-resolution photography, mismatched color systems, awkward spacing all read as "small / amateur / risky" — even when the underlying business is excellent.

The flip side: clean, considered design earns the benefit of the doubt. The prospect treats subsequent claims as more credible because the visual presentation has already established competence.

This is a credibility transfer rule (`psych-transfer-credibility.md` adjacent) — design quality borrows credibility from the *category of professionalism* and applies it to your specific brand.

### What "professional design" means in practice

- **Consistent type hierarchy** (`layout-typefaces.md`).
- **Aligned grid** — text, images, buttons land on consistent vertical and horizontal lines.
- **Restrained color palette** (`layout-color.md`) — 2-3 colors plus neutrals, applied with intent.
- **High-quality photography** — original shots if possible; well-chosen stock if not. Avoid the obvious stock photos.
- **Real white space** (`layout-white-space.md`) — not "empty" but breathing room.
- **Modern tooling** — current web standards, mobile-responsive, fast loading.
- **Considered details** — button states, micro-typography, image cropping.

### Common mistakes

- **DIY design when the budget exists for a professional** — the highest-leverage spend you can make on a new brand or campaign.
- **Treating design as decoration** — design IS persuasion machinery, not finishing polish.
- **Hiring cheap because "design is design"** — the gap between competent and great design is the same gap as competent and great copy. Both are skill-dependent.
- **Letting non-designers make design decisions** — leadership review is fine; leadership control of design choices usually isn't.
- **Inconsistent design across surfaces** — strong landing page, weak email, ugly invoice. The weakest surface is the perception ceiling.

### When to apply

- Every customer-facing surface for a brand making credibility claims.
- New brands especially — you have no track record to fall back on; design has to do that work.
- Premium positioning — design is non-negotiable.
- Higher-ticket purchases — design quality must match price expectation.

### When NOT to apply

- Categories where deliberately raw / DIY design is the brand voice (some indie communities, some art categories).
- Internal/B2B contexts where the audience explicitly values function over polish.
