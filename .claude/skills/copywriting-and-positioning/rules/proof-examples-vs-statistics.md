## Lead with examples; use statistics as supporting evidence — not the other way around

**Impact: HIGH** — Examples and statistics both work, but they work *differently*. Examples create a vivid mental scene the prospect inserts themselves into. Statistics validate the example logically. The winning sequence is example → statistic, not statistic → example.

Why examples beat statistics for the *opening* job:

1. **Self-demonstration** — well-written examples cause the prospect to mentally play out using the product, building desire automatically.
2. **Lower cognitive load** — examples relate to personal experience. Statistics require abstract reasoning.
3. **Emotional engagement** — examples carry sensory and emotional content statistics can't.

Statistics are still essential — they back the example up, especially for skeptical or central-route audiences (`psych-attitude-routes.md`). But statistics opening the pitch loses to examples opening the pitch in nearly every test.

**Match the leading-evidence type to the product:**

| Product type | Lead with… | Use stats for… |
|---|---|---|
| Beer | Examples — attractive people, good times, sensory scenes | Almost nothing. Pure peripheral route. |
| Cars | Examples — driving scenes, lifestyle | Performance / safety / efficiency stats for the segments that care |
| Laser printers | Stats first (utilitarian) | Examples are weaker; spec-sheet purchase. |
| Landscape services | Examples — beautiful yards, neighbor envy, weekend freedom | Light stats on equipment, frequency. |
| Gym memberships | Examples — before/afters, photos, testimonial scenes | Light stats on equipment count, square footage. |
| Enterprise software for engineers | Stats first (central route) — see `psych-attitude-routes.md` | Examples support: case studies, named customers. |

**Don't write this — leading with statistics for an emotion-driven product:**

```text
Cruise vacation ad:

"Our cruise line operates 14 ships across 47 itineraries with
an average passenger satisfaction rating of 4.7/5 across 184,000
verified reviews. Cabin sizes range from 175-2,550 square feet.
We offer 23 dining options and 31 entertainment venues per ship."
```

Why it fails: the prospect isn't deciding based on cabin square footage. They're deciding based on whether they can *see themselves* on the ship. The stats are useful — but they're answering a question that hasn't been asked yet.

**Write this instead — example first, stats supporting:**

```text
Cruise vacation ad:

"Picture this: 7am. You're on the private balcony of your suite,
robe wrapped around you, the smell of fresh espresso your butler
just delivered, watching the morning sun light up the cliffs of
Santorini as the ship glides into port.

By 10am you're walking the white-washed alleys with a local
guide who's done this for 20 years. By dinner you're back on
board for the chef's table — the same chef who ran a Michelin
two-star in Lyon for a decade.

That's day three of fourteen.

Our 14 ships across 47 itineraries average 4.7/5 satisfaction
across 184,000 verified reviews. Cabins range from 175 to 2,550
sq ft. 23 dining options. 31 entertainment venues per ship."
```

Why it works:
- **Example builds the mental movie first** (`body-mental-movies.md`).
- **Self-demonstration triggered** — prospect sees themselves on the balcony.
- **Stats follow as supporting evidence** — for the prospect who needs validation after the desire is built.
- **Both audiences served** — peripheral-route reader sells themselves on the example; central-route reader gets the stats they need.

### Common mistakes

- **Statistics-only copy** for emotional categories — leaves the desire unbuilt.
- **Examples-only copy** for utilitarian categories — leaves the buyer's logical justification unsatisfied.
- **Stats with no examples for B2C** — even B2B central-route audiences usually want a customer story to make the abstract concrete.
- **Examples without stats for high-stakes purchases** — buyer's internal justification (or to their boss) will need numbers.
- **Generic examples** — examples need the specificity of `body-extreme-specificity.md` to land. "A typical customer enjoyed our product" is not an example.

### When to apply

- Default sequence in every long-form piece: lead example → support stats.
- Emotional / lifestyle / aspirational products → lean heavily on examples.
- Hero sections of landing pages — example first.

### When NOT to apply

- Pure technical specs documents where the audience is comparing on a spreadsheet (architectural diagrams, datasheets — these are explicitly stats-first by purpose).
- When you don't have a vivid example yet — better no example than a weak one. Build the example first.
