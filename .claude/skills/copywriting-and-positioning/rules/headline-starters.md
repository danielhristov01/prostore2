## Use one of the 22 psychologically-tested headline starters when stuck

**Impact: HIGH** — A headline must do two things: grab attention AND motivate continued reading. These 22 opening patterns have been refined over decades of direct-response testing. Each one triggers a specific psychological response — curiosity, urgency, novelty, identification.

These are *starters*, not *templates*. The job of the headline is still to deliver the biggest benefit (`headline-biggest-benefit.md`). The starter is the on-ramp; the benefit is the road.

### The 22 starters

| # | Starter | Trigger | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **FREE** | Reciprocation, value | "Free Book Shows You How to Write Sneaky Advertising That Practically Forces People to Send You Money!" |
| 2 | **NEW** | Novelty, curiosity | "Powerful New Seminar Teaches Flea Marketers the Power of 'Flea-Psych'…" |
| 3 | **AT LAST** | Relief, anticipation | "At Last…A Bakery That Uses Only Organic Sugar, Flour, Milk, and Eggs!" |
| 4 | **THIS** | Specificity, immediacy | "This New Invention Stops Any Attacker in His Tracks Without a Gun, Knife, or Black Belt." |
| 5 | **ANNOUNCING** | Newsworthiness | "Announcing the Hottest New Sandwich Craze From Southern California: The Malibu Crust Pocket!" |
| 6 | **WARNING!** | Fear, attention spike | "WARNING! Some Dog Groomers Wrap a Noose Around Your Dog's Neck!" |
| 7 | **JUST RELEASED** | Newness, exclusivity | "Just Released: Psychologist's Study Reveals Little-Known Speaking Patterns…" |
| 8 | **NOW** | Immediacy, capability | "Now You Can Stop Any Attacker Without Guns, Knives, or a Black Belt." |
| 9 | **HERE'S** | Direct, conversational | "Here's How a 95-Pound Granny Made a 275-Pound Psychopathic Killer Cry Like a Baby…" |
| 10 | **THESE** | Specificity, demonstrative | "These Three Very Italian Men Make a Pizza to Kill For." |
| 11 | **WHICH OF** | Self-identification | "Which of These Hot Bodies Would YOU Like to Show Off?" |
| 12 | **FINALLY** | Relief, anticipation | "Finally…a Self-Improvement Seminar That Moves, Empowers, and Transforms You for Life!" |
| 13 | **LOOK** | Attention demand | "LOOK! Now You Can Buy Cotton Candy Machines at Wholesale Prices." |
| 14 | **PRESENTING** | Authority, formality | "Presenting the Easiest Way Ever Developed to Learn the Piano." |
| 15 | **INTRODUCING** | Novelty, exclusivity | "Introducing the Only Water Ice Stand in Philly that Uses Real Fresh Fruit." |
| 16 | **HOW** | Method, learning | "How to Sing Like an American Idol in 90 Days or Less—Guaranteed." |
| 17 | **AMAZING** | Curiosity (use sparingly) | "Amazing New DVD Lowers Your Blood Pressure by Just Watching It!" |
| 18 | **DO YOU** | Engagement, self-question | "Do You Know How to Stop Vicious Dog Attacks with the Push of a Button?" |
| 19 | **WOULD YOU** | Hypothetical, transaction | "Would You Trade $2 for Our Famous Brick-Oven Pizza?" |
| 20 | **CAN YOU** | Capability, challenge | "Can You Be Sure Your Child Won't Get Kidnapped?" |
| 21 | **IF YOU** | Conditional, audience-targeting | "If You Hate Cleaning Your Pool, This Ad Brings Good News!" |
| 22 | **STARTING TODAY** | Immediacy, transformation | "Starting Today You Can Dance 97% Better…If You Follow These Rules." |

**Don't write this — generic non-starter headline:**

```text
Headline: Our knife sharpening service is excellent.
```

Why it fails: no opener trigger, no benefit, no curiosity hook.

**Write this instead — pick a starter that fits the audience and benefit:**

```text
Starter 16 (HOW):
"How a Restaurant Chef Sharpens 80 Knives a Week in 4 Minutes
Each — Without Any Skill."

Starter 9 (HERE'S):
"Here's the 4-Minute Knife Sharpening Method Three Michelin
Restaurants Switched To Last Year."

Starter 6 (WARNING):
"WARNING: Most 'Sharpening' Services Round Off Your Blade by
0.3mm. Here's How to Tell If Yours Did."
```

Each works differently — different audiences will respond to different starters. A/B test if the budget allows.

### How to pick the right starter

- **Curiosity-driven categories** → HOW, WHICH OF, THIS, THESE, HERE'S
- **Fear/safety categories** → WARNING, CAN YOU, DO YOU
- **Novelty/launches** → NEW, ANNOUNCING, INTRODUCING, JUST RELEASED, PRESENTING, AT LAST, FINALLY
- **Identification/segmentation** → IF YOU, WHICH OF, WOULD YOU, CAN YOU
- **Capability promises** → NOW, STARTING TODAY, HOW
- **Free-offer-driven** → FREE
- **Stop-the-scroll urgency** → LOOK, AMAZING (use sparingly — overused)

### Common mistakes

- **Starter without a real benefit** — "INTRODUCING our new logo!" is using the starter slot but not delivering. Starter + benefit, always together.
- **Cutesy use of WARNING / AMAZING** for trivial topics — diminishes the trigger. Save high-attention starters for content that earns them.
- **Don't get cutesy or clever with headlines that don't target the right audience** simply to hook eyeballs. Generic curiosity bait pulls clicks but fails to convert. Targeted curiosity wins.
- **Treating the list as 22 ideas instead of 22 patterns** — these are tested triggers; modify the example, don't replace the trigger word arbitrarily.

### When to apply

- Every time you're stuck on a headline.
- A/B testing — generate three different starters for the same benefit.
- Email subject lines and ad headlines.

### When NOT to apply

- When you have a stronger custom headline that doesn't fit any starter — these are crutches, not requirements.
- Premium luxury where understatement is the brand voice — many starters read as too direct-response for that register.
