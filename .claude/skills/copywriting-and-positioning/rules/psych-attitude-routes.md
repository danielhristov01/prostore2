## Match the persuasion route — central (logic) or peripheral (cues) — to the audience and product

**Impact: HIGH** — The Elaboration Likelihood Model: people process persuasive messages two ways. Pick the wrong route and your best ammunition lands as noise.

| Route | How prospect processes | What works |
|---|---|---|
| **Central** | Deep thinking, weighing facts, comparing arguments | Stats, evidence, testimonials, studies, reports, case histories, comparison tables |
| **Peripheral** | Shortcut cues, surface signals, vibes, emotion | Pleasant images, humor, celebrity/sponsor cues, color, music, attractive people, social proof at-a-glance |

What determines which route the prospect uses?

- **Motivation** — high stakes, personal relevance, money on the line → central. Low stakes, low relevance → peripheral.
- **Ability** — knowledgeable, time to think → central. Distracted, busy, unfamiliar → peripheral.
- **Product** — utilitarian/B2B/considered → central. Hedonic/impulse/lifestyle → peripheral.

A bonus from the central route: when someone is persuaded by their own deep thinking, they treat the conclusion as their own brainchild and *defend* it against future attacks (see `psych-inoculation.md`). Central-route persuasion is stickier than peripheral.

**Don't write this — peripheral cues for a high-stakes central audience:**

```text
B2B SaaS landing page for CTOs:

Hero: [Smiling diverse team in sunlit office]
Headline: Workflows that just feel right.
Body: Our customers are the most innovative companies in tech.
Built with love. ❤️
[Watch our 30-second brand video]
```

Why it fails: CTO evaluating a tool is in central mode. They want architecture diagrams, latency numbers, security pages, customer logos with engineering context, and pricing. Vibe-marketing reads as evasion.

**Write this instead — central-route material for a central-route audience:**

```text
B2B SaaS landing page for CTOs:

Hero headline: Sub-50ms p95 latency, SOC 2 Type II, on-prem
deployment available.

Body: Built for engineering teams who need their workflow
platform to handle 10K+ events/sec without a tier upgrade.
Used by infra teams at Stripe, Vercel, and Linear (case study
links below).

Architecture diagram: [actual diagram]
Pricing: [actual pricing table]
Security: [SOC 2, GDPR, HIPAA compliance details]
Comparison with [competitor]: [actual comparison table]
```

Why it works:
- Audience is in central mode → fed central material (specs, evidence, comparison).
- Vibe is implied by the *quality* of the technical content, not asserted.
- Even peripheral cues that work for this audience (Stripe/Vercel/Linear logos) function as *evidence* (proof of capability), not just emotion.

**The mirror case — central-route content for a peripheral audience:**

```text
DON'T (peripheral audience, central content):
Impulse-buy snack ad in subway:
"Our potato chips contain 23% less sodium than the leading
competitor according to a 2024 lab analysis. View our white
paper on sodium reduction techniques here."

DO (peripheral audience, peripheral content):
"That salty crunch you crave. Without the bloat tomorrow."
[Photo of chips + happy person]
```

### Common mistakes

- **Defaulting to one route across all audiences** — the same brand needs central-route material for procurement and peripheral material for end-users.
- **Mixing routes badly** — vibe-heavy hero with deep technical body. Pick a primary route per page section; layer the other route as support.
- **Underestimating central audiences** — pretty pictures don't sell to engineers, lawyers, doctors, scientists *for their professional purchases*. (They do sell for their personal lives.)
- **Overestimating central audiences** — even sophisticated buyers use peripheral cues for surface judgments. A central-route audience with a broken-looking website still bounces.

### When to apply

- Always when designing copy: first ask "which route is this audience in for THIS decision?"
- Page-by-page in a funnel: top of funnel often peripheral, bottom of funnel often central.
- Audience research output should map to route preference.

### When NOT to apply

- N/A — every persuasive piece must answer the route question. The rule itself always applies.
