# Copywriting & Positioning

Direct-response copywriting and positioning guide. Rules are organized by the persuasion job they do, not by the book they came from. Apply CRITICAL/HIGH-impact rules first when reviewing or writing copy.

## When to apply

Reference these rules when the task involves:

- Writing or reviewing ad copy (print, social, search, display)
- Writing or reviewing website copy (hero, sections, sales pages, emails)
- Crafting a USP, value proposition, or positioning statement
- Designing offers (pricing, guarantees, scarcity, bonuses)
- Reviewing a layout for response/conversion (typography, color, white space, picture-headline-body hierarchy)
- Diagnosing why copy isn't converting

Do NOT use this skill for: brand voice essays, long-form thought leadership, or content marketing where the goal is awareness rather than response. The rules here optimize for *action*, not *impression*.

## Quick reference

Read individual rule files (`rules/<prefix>-<name>.md`) for full explanations and copy examples.

### 1. Psychology of motivation (CRITICAL)

- `psych-lf8` — Tie every appeal to one of the eight Life-Force wants (foundation rule)
- `psych-fear` — Use fear to make the threat real, then offer the specific solution
- `psych-ego` — Use ego-morphing — make the product part of the prospect's identity
- `psych-transfer-credibility` — Borrow credibility by associating the product with respected symbols, people, or institutions
- `psych-social-beings` — Anchor the product to a reference group (aspirational, associative, dissociative)
- `psych-means-end-chain` — Sell the end-state, not the product — ladder features up to ultimate benefit
- `psych-prospect-stages` — Match the copy to the prospect's awareness stage (precontemplation → maintenance)
- `psych-inoculation` — Inoculate loyal customers with weak attacks so they defend you against competitors
- `psych-change-their-reality` — Shift focus or importance of beliefs — don't try to overturn them
- `psych-attitude-routes` — Match persuasion route (central / peripheral) to audience and product
- `psych-weapons-of-influence` — Stack Cialdini's six weapons — comparison, liking, authority, reciprocation, commitment, scarcity
- `psych-heuristics` — Trigger the Length-Implies-Strength heuristic — volume signals quality

### 2. Headline craft (CRITICAL)

- `headline-biggest-benefit` — Put the biggest benefit in the headline — without exception
- `headline-starters` — Use one of the 22 psychologically-tested headline starters when stuck
- `headline-power-of-questions` — Open with a question to install an "open loop" the brain has to close
- `headline-guillotine` — Use a human face — especially eyes — as the dominant visual to grab attention

### 3. Offer construction (HIGH)

- `offer-usp` — Establish a Unique Selling Proposition the audience can't get from any competitor
- `offer-scarcity` — Add a deadline or limited quantity to break inertia
- `offer-guarantees` — Offer a long, strong, specific guarantee — longer than competitors'
- `offer-pricing` — Choose odd-cent pricing for value, round-number pricing for prestige

### 4. Proof & credibility (HIGH)

- `proof-evidence` — Back every claim with evidence you didn't create yourself
- `proof-social-proof` — Pile on testimonials — named, photographed, specific, in volume
- `proof-examples-vs-statistics` — Lead with examples; use statistics as supporting evidence

### 5. Body copy craft (HIGH)

- `body-bombard-with-benefits` — Translate every feature into a benefit, then pile them on
- `body-extreme-specificity` — Be extremely specific — exact numbers, named items, counted lists
- `body-mental-movies` — Direct mental movies by engaging all five senses (V-A-K-O-G)
- `body-power-visual-adjectives` — Use Power Visual Adjectives (PVAs) to render the product vividly
- `body-granny-rule` — Write the way a salesperson would talk — meet them in their world
- `body-long-vs-short` — Long copy outsells short when the prospect is genuinely interested
- `body-sales-letter-in-survey` — Disguise an offer inside a customer survey to bypass ad-resistance
- `body-cleverectomy` — Cut clever for clear — clever copy that doesn't sell is bad copy

### 6. Call-to-action (HIGH)

- `cta-battling-inertia` — Push for action — make it easy to act, then explicitly ask

### 7. Message structure (MEDIUM)

- `structure-simplicity` — Write to the chimpanzee brain — short words, sentences, paragraphs, "you"
- `structure-message-organization` — Organize the message — clear structure outranks clever content
- `structure-message-sidedness` — Use two-sided messaging — acknowledge competitors before differentiating
- `structure-repetition` — Use redundancy (varied repetition), not raw repetition
- `structure-rhetorical-questions` — Use rhetorical questions for retention, not as primary persuasion

### 8. Layout & design (MEDIUM)

- `layout-typefaces` — Set headlines in initial caps; serif for print body, sans-serif for web body
- `layout-pictures` — Use product-in-use photos — they outpull product-only and text-only ads
- `layout-white-space` — Wrap ads in white space — buy bigger, but don't fill it
- `layout-color` — Color affects perceived weight, taste, and brand association
- `layout-color-preferences` — Use color (60% more readership than B&W); blue/red/green lead in preference
- `layout-size` — Bigger ads attract more attention — but at diminishing returns vs. cost
- `layout-pro-design` — Invest in professional design — graphics quality is a credibility signal

## How to use

When writing or reviewing copy:

1. **Identify the persuasion job.** Is the copy trying to grab attention (headline)? Build desire (body)? Prove credibility (proof)? Push action (cta)?
2. **Load the relevant prefix.** Read `rules/<prefix>-*.md` for the rules in that category.
3. **Apply CRITICAL/HIGH first.** Prioritize psychology + headline + offer + proof + body over micro-optimizations like color choice.
4. **Cross-reference.** Each rule links to related rules — follow them for layered application.

### Suggested review order for a piece of copy

When auditing existing copy, work top-to-bottom through these checks:

1. **Foundation** — does it ladder to an LF8 want? (`psych-lf8`)
2. **USP** — is the differentiation clear? (`offer-usp`)
3. **Headline** — biggest benefit, specific, not clever? (`headline-biggest-benefit`, `body-cleverectomy`)
4. **Structure** — AIDA followed? (`structure-message-organization`, `body-granny-rule`)
5. **Body** — features translated to benefits? Specific? Sensory? (`body-bombard-with-benefits`, `body-extreme-specificity`, `body-mental-movies`)
6. **Proof** — evidence for every claim? Social proof in volume? (`proof-evidence`, `proof-social-proof`)
7. **Offer** — guarantee, scarcity, pricing format match positioning? (`offer-guarantees`, `offer-scarcity`, `offer-pricing`)
8. **CTA** — explicit ask, easy to act, scarcity pairing? (`cta-battling-inertia`)
9. **Layout** — typography, color, white space, in-use photos? (`layout-*`)

## Rule file format

Every rule file under `rules/` follows the format in `rules/_template.md`. Frontmatter declares title, impact level, tags, persuasion stage, applies-to surfaces, and source. Body contains: brief explanation → **Don't write this** copy example → **Write this instead** copy example → common mistakes → when to apply / when NOT to apply → references to related rules.

Files prefixed with underscore (`_template.md`) are utilities, not rules.
